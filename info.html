About the IWASEE Workshop

About IWASEE

Modern engineering systems — from energy-aware computing infrastructures to autonomous robots and connected IoT networks — face a defining challenge: as they grow in complexity, ensuring their efficiency, sustainability, and reliability becomes exponentially harder. Advances in artificial intelligence, green computing, and wireless systems are reshaping what is possible, yet academic communities in Latin America often lack dedicated venues for deep, interdisciplinary exchange.

IWASEE was born from the conviction that bridging green computing, intelligent automation, and wireless systems engineering is not just academically valuable — it is essential for the next generation of efficient, sustainable, and connected systems.

Initiated by the KR – Knowledge Representation research group in Peru, in collaboration with researchers across Latin America, IWASEE creates a focused, high-quality forum where practitioners and researchers can present cutting-edge work, engage in rigorous discussion, and forge lasting international collaborations.

Mission & Vision

Mission

IWASEE's mission is to provide an international, interdisciplinary forum for the exchange of scientific advances at the intersection of green computing, robotics and automation, and wireless systems engineering. We connect researchers and engineers to share novel approaches that improve how intelligent, efficient, and connected systems are designed and deployed.

Vision

We envision IWASEE as the leading Latin American workshop for advanced systems efficiency, recognized internationally for fostering transformative research across its three core tracks. Our long-term goal is a collaborative research community where green computing, autonomous systems, and connected infrastructure converge — producing solutions that are not only capable, but sustainable and efficient by design.
